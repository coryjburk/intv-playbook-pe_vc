## 1. Welcome

The **Private Equity & Value Creation Interview Playbook** gives Eccles MBA candidates a technically deep, on-demand resource for the parts of PE interviews that reward repetition: three-statement mechanics, LBO math, value-creation levers, and crisp executive communication.

It is designed to complement — not replace — the coaching your advisory team provides. Where your coaches focus on behavioral (BBI/CARL) and introduction work, this playbook is the self-service technical companion you can use any time.

> [!NOTE]
> **What you can do with it**
> - **Study** 100 interview questions across both the Investment and Portfolio Operations tracks, each with a model answer, a technical deep-dive, and an Eccles coaching note.
> - **Practice** out loud or by typing in the simulator, then submit your answer for an instant 0–100 evaluation.
> - **Track** your readiness across twelve competencies and watch your overall score climb as you improve.

## 4. The Question Bank

This is where you will spend most of your time. The bank holds **100 questions, split evenly — 50 Investment and 50 Portfolio Operations** — across twelve competency categories.

### Filtering

Three dropdowns at the top narrow the list:

- **Track** — All, Investment, or Portfolio Operations. Choosing a track automatically updates the Category dropdown to show only that track's categories, so you never land on an empty result.
- **Category** — the competency area (e.g., Leveraged Buyouts, Value Creation, Board & Executive Communication).
- **Difficulty** — Foundational, Core, Advanced, or Expert. Build up the ladder as your confidence grows.

### Anatomy of a question card

Click any question to expand it. Each card carries four tabs:

| Tab | What it gives you |
| --- | --- |
| **Recruiter Intent** | The competency being tested and why an interviewer asks it — so you answer the real question behind the question. |
| **Model Answer** | A concise, interview-ready response structure, plus a highlighted red flag to avoid. |
| **Technical Deep-Dive** | The extra layer of rigor that separates a strong answer from an elite one. |
| **Eccles Coaching Note** | A delivery tip from your advisory team on how to frame and land the point. |

Every card also has a **Launch Interactive Practice Simulator** button, which is where the real rehearsal happens.

## 5. The Practice Simulator

The simulator gives you a clean space to answer a single question, by voice or by typing, and then have your response evaluated.

### Voice or type

- **Type your answer** in the response box at any time. This always works, on any device, and is scored exactly the same as a spoken answer.
- **Speak your answer** by clicking the microphone. Your speech is transcribed live into the box, and on-screen metrics track your pace (words per minute), filler words, and duration as you go.

> [!WARNING]
> **Why the microphone sometimes says "unavailable"**
> Browser security only allows microphone access on a secure page opened in its own tab. Voice will work on the **published `https` link, in Chrome or Edge, in a normal browser tab**.
>
> It will **not** work if you open the file directly from your computer, or inside an embedded preview window. When that happens, the tool tells you why — and you simply **type your answer instead**. The evaluation is identical.

### Three buttons

- **Submit for AI Evaluation** — scores your answer and updates your dashboard (see the next section).
- **Simulate Perfect Response** — loads the model answer into the box and scores it, so you can see what a top-tier response looks like on the rubric.
- **Copy Prompt for Live AI Coach** — generates a ready-made prompt you can paste into a live AI assistant (such as Claude or ChatGPT) for richer, conversational feedback on your specific answer.

## 6. AI Evaluation & Scoring

When you click **Submit for AI Evaluation**, the tool grades your answer from 0 to 100 and shows a breakdown across four axes:

| Axis | Weight | What it measures |
| --- | :---: | --- |
| **Content** | 45% | Overlap with the core frameworks and vocabulary the question expects (e.g., EBITDA, leverage, working capital, value-creation levers). |
| **Structure** | 22% | Whether your answer sits in a strong length band (roughly 60–180 words) and uses logical connectors. |
| **Specificity** | 18% | Quantification — dollar figures, percentages, and multiples. PE answers live on numbers. |
| **Delivery** | 15% | Filler-word control. Frequent "um / like / basically" pulls this down. |

Your overall score maps to a readiness tier:

| Score | Tier |
| --- | --- |
| 90 – 100 | **Elite** |
| 75 – 89 | **Interview Ready** |
| 55 – 74 | **Strong** |
| 30 – 54 | **Competitive** |
| 0 – 29 | **Emerging** |

Below the score you will see which frameworks you **hit** (green) and which the model answer **expected** (red), plus short coaching notes drawn from your weakest axes — a concrete to-do list for your next attempt.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Honest labeling: what the "AI Evaluation" really is**
> The in-tool score is a fast, transparent **heuristic** — it analyzes keyword overlap, length, quantification, and filler density. It is excellent for spotting trends and self-coaching, but it is not a human interviewer and the exact number will vary slightly.
>
> For genuine, nuanced AI feedback, use **Copy Prompt for Live AI Coach** and paste the prompt into a real AI assistant. That is the true "AI" layer; treat the on-screen score as a practice gauge, not a verdict.

## 7. The Readiness Dashboard

Every evaluation feeds the dashboard. Each of the twelve competency categories carries a 0–100 bar, and your **Overall PE Readiness** score is the average across all twelve.

- The tool keeps your **best demonstrated score** in each category, so a quick rough attempt never erases earlier strong work.
- Your overall tier badge (Emerging through Elite) updates the moment a category improves.
- **Your progress saves automatically** in your browser after each evaluation, so you can close the tab and pick up where you left off on another day. A status line under the score shows when it was last updated.
- To wipe the slate and start over, use the **Reset Dashboard to Zero** button on the dashboard. It asks for confirmation, then clears all twelve competencies and removes the saved data.
- Aim to lift every bar above 75 (Interview Ready) before a live interview, paying special attention to the categories matching the role you are recruiting for.

> [!NOTE]
> **About saved progress**
> Your scores are saved in the **specific browser on the specific device** you practice on — there is no login or cloud sync. Progress carries across days on the same machine and browser, but it will not follow you to a different device or browser. Everything stays private to you.

## 8. Frameworks, Battlecards & Red Flags

Three reference modules round out the playbook:

- **Strategic Frameworks** — sixteen institutional frameworks split across the two tracks. Use them to structure an answer when a question feels open-ended; select one from the left menu to read its core mechanics and application guidance.
- **Interview Battlecards** — two condensed cards (Investment and Portfolio Operations) with must-know concepts, power phrases, metrics to know cold, and mental-math shortcuts. Skim these the morning of an interview.
- **Deal Team Red Flags** — ten classic ways candidates lose credibility, each with an Eccles remedy. Read them as a pre-flight checklist of what not to do.

## 9. A Recommended Practice Routine

A simple loop that builds real readiness:

1. **Pick a lane.** Filter to the track and category that matches your target role (or your weakest dashboard bar).
2. **Read before you rehearse.** Expand a question and read the Recruiter Intent and Model Answer tabs so you know the target.
3. **Answer cold.** Launch the simulator and respond out loud (or typed) without looking back — this mirrors the real moment.
4. **Submit and read the feedback.** Note your weakest axis and the missing frameworks.
5. **Run it again.** Re-answer, working in the missing vocabulary and a number or two. Watch the score move.
6. **Go deep occasionally.** For high-stakes questions, use Copy Prompt for Live AI Coach to get a conversational critique from a live assistant.
7. **Check the dashboard.** End each session by glancing at which bars still sit below Interview Ready, and start there next time.

## 10. FAQ & Troubleshooting

<details>
<summary><strong>The microphone won't record.</strong></summary>

Open the published `https` link in Chrome or Edge, in its own tab, and allow microphone access when prompted. Voice cannot work from a local file or an embedded preview — type your answer instead; it scores the same.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How do I clear my progress and start over?</strong></summary>

Open the Readiness Dashboard and click **Reset Dashboard to Zero**. It confirms first, then clears all twelve competencies and deletes the saved data for this browser. Otherwise your progress saves automatically and persists across sessions — it will not reset on its own when you refresh or close the tab.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>My progress didn't appear on another device or browser.</strong></summary>

Saved progress is tied to the specific browser and device you practiced on; it does not sync across devices. Use the same browser on the same machine to see your running dashboard.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>The score feels approximate.</strong></summary>

It is a transparent heuristic meant for trend-spotting and self-coaching, not precise grading. For nuanced feedback, use **Copy Prompt for Live AI Coach**.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Will other students see my answers or scores?</strong></summary>

No. Everything runs privately in your own browser; your saved scores never leave your device and nothing is shared.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>A category shows no questions.</strong></summary>

Make sure your Track and Difficulty filters aren't contradicting each other. Resetting all three dropdowns to "All" shows the full bank.

</details>
